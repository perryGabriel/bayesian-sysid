## Shared model and simulation assumptions

Across the demos, the core workflow is:

1. Simulate or construct data from a known ARX-style process.
2. Fit a Bayesian ARX model (`BayesianARX`) to estimate posterior over parameters.
3. Propagate posterior uncertainty into predictions, trajectories, frequency response, stability, or control metrics.

### Core methods in `src/`

- Bayesian/LS ARX fitting and posterior sampling:
  - `src/bayes_sysid/models.py`
  - Key functions/classes: `BayesianARX.fit`, `BayesianARX.sample_parameters`, `BayesianARX.rollout_posterior_samples`, `LeastSquaresARX`.
- Frequency response + posterior bands:
  - `src/bayes_sysid/analysis/frequency_response.py`
  - Key functions: `arx_frequency_response`, `posterior_frequency_response_samples`, `posterior_magnitude_envelope`.
- Stability and pole-based probability of stability:
  - `src/bayes_sysid/analysis/stability.py`
  - Key functions: `arx_poles`, `posterior_stability_probability`.
- Closed-loop Monte Carlo simulation under posterior uncertainty:
  - `src/bayes_sysid/control/closed_loop.py`
  - Key functions: `simulate_closed_loop_arx`, `monte_carlo_closed_loop_paths`.
- Classical and empirical margin summaries:
  - `src/bayes_sysid/control/margins.py`
  - Key functions: `classical_margins_from_open_loop`, `empirical_margin_report`.
- DSF scaffold and validation helpers:
  - `src/bayes_sysid/control/dsf.py`
  - Key functions: `dsf_from_transfer_matrix`, `posterior_edge_probability`, `validate_identifiability_assumptions`, `validate_excitation_richness`.

## Artifact-by-artifact interpretation

## 1) `predictive_density_annotated.png`

**Caption-ready interpretation:**
Posterior predictive density for one next-step output, showing that the observed output, least-squares point prediction, Bayesian posterior mean, and true conditional mean can all be mutually consistent when uncertainty is represented as a full predictive distribution instead of a single point estimate.

**Generated by:** `examples/demo_arx.py`

**Generating process:**
Synthetic stable ARX(2,2) data with Gaussian noise is simulated (`a_true=[0.55,-0.18]`, `b_true=[0.90,0.25]`, `sigma=0.20`), then LS and Bayesian ARX models are fit on the same sequence.

**Method locations:**
`src/bayes_sysid/models.py` (`predict_next_distribution`, `predictive_density_grid`).

---

## 2) `rollout_teacher_forcing_vs_recursive.png`

**Caption-ready interpretation:**
Comparison of one-step-ahead (teacher-forced) versus recursive free-run predictions for LS and Bayesian posterior-mean models; one-step predictions remain anchored to true history while recursive rollouts accumulate model error over horizon.

**Generated by:** `examples/demo_arx.py`

**Generating process:**
Same ARX(2,2) synthetic dataset and fitted models as above; horizon segment extracted near the trajectory end and predicted using two rollout protocols.

**Method locations:**
`src/bayes_sysid/models.py` (`LeastSquaresARX.predict_next`, `LeastSquaresARX.simulate_one_step_rollout`, `BayesianARX.posterior_mean_prediction`).

---

## 3) `posterior_trajectory_band.png`

**Caption-ready interpretation:**
Free-run multi-step posterior trajectory band (10-90%) from parameter and process uncertainty, with observed trajectory overlaid to show probabilistic forecast spread.

**Generated by:** `examples/demo_arx.py`

**Generating process:**
Posterior parameter samples are drawn after fitting Bayesian ARX; each sample is recursively rolled out with optional process noise, then quantiles are summarized.

**Method locations:**
`src/bayes_sysid/models.py` (`rollout_posterior_samples`).

---

## 4) `sub_nyquist_rate.png`

**Caption-ready interpretation:**
Predictive uncertainty widens and temporal detail is distorted under aggressive downsampling (proxy for sub-Nyquist/aliasing conditions), compared with a densely sampled baseline.

**Generated by:** `examples/demo_uncertainty_insufficient_information.py`

**Generating process:**
A high-rate input/output pair is generated from a stable scalar ARX process, then downsampled by factor 6; separate Bayesian ARX models are fit to dense and sparse datasets and compared via 90% rollout bands.

**Method locations:**
`src/bayes_sysid/models.py` (`BayesianARX.fit`, `rollout_posterior_samples`).

---

## 5) `underconstrained_data.png`

**Caption-ready interpretation:**
With very limited noisy data, posterior predictive bands are substantially broader than with a large dataset, illustrating uncertainty inflation from underconstrained identification.

**Generated by:** `examples/demo_uncertainty_insufficient_information.py`

**Generating process:**
Two Bayesian ARX fits are compared (`n_small=10` vs. `n_large=800`) on data from the same noisy synthetic process; each model produces a posterior rollout band over the same horizon.

**Method locations:**
`src/bayes_sysid/models.py` (`BayesianARX.fit`, `rollout_posterior_samples`).

## 9) `nyquist_posterior_band.png`

## Reproducibility notes

- Re-run any producing script from repository root, e.g.:
  - `python examples/demo_arx.py`
  - `python examples/demo_uncertainty_insufficient_information.py`
  - `python examples/demo_stability_and_robustness.py`
  - `python examples/demo_posterior_nyquist_band.py`
  - `python examples/demo_dsf_scaffold.py`
- Most scripts set deterministic seeds via `numpy.random.default_rng(...)` and/or explicit `random_state=` values for reproducible artifacts.
- All figures/tables above are written into `examples/artifacts/` (or `examples/artifacts/dsf/`).
